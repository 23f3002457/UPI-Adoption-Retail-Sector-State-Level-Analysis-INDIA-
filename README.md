# Digital Payment Adoption and Retail Sector Growth in India
### State-wise UPI Analysis

---

## Overview

The Unified Payments Interface (UPI) has become one of the most widely used digital payment systems in India since its launch in 2016. With the rapid growth of digital transactions, it is often assumed that increased digital payment adoption leads to stronger retail sector growth.

This project examines whether differences in **UPI transaction activity across Indian states** correspond with variations in **Retail Gross State Value Added (GSVA)**. The goal is to understand whether higher digital payment usage is reflected in stronger retail sector performance across states.

This work was completed as part of the **Business Data Management (BDM) Capstone Project in the IIT Madras BS Degree Program**.

---

## Research Objectives

The project addresses the following questions:

1. **To examine whether state-wise UPI transaction activity corresponds with the growth of the retail sector (Retail GSVA).**

2. **To identify states where retail sector growth exists but digital payment adoption remains relatively lower.**

---

## Data Sources

All datasets used in this project are publicly available secondary data from official government repositories.

### Retail GSVA Data
- **Source:** Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation (MoSPI)
- **Sector Used:** Trade, Repair, Hotels & Restaurants (used as proxy for retail sector)
- **Time Period:** Financial Year 2011–12 to 2023–24

### UPI Transaction Data
- **Source:** National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI)
- **Data Used:**
  - National monthly and annual UPI transaction statistics
  - State-wise UPI transaction value for FY 2023–24

**Official Sources**

- https://www.mospi.gov.in  
- https://www.npci.org.in

## Methodology

The analysis follows a descriptive and exploratory approach using spreadsheet-based tools.

Key steps in the analysis include:

- Organizing Retail GSVA data across states and years
- Calculating long-term GSVA growth for each state
- Aggregating monthly UPI transaction data into annual totals
- Computing each state's share of national UPI transaction value
- Comparing digital payment intensity with retail sector growth

States were evaluated using three key dimensions:

- **Retail GSVA Size**
- **Retail GSVA Growth Rate**
- **UPI Transaction Share**

---

## State Classification Framework

Based on the combined analysis, states were grouped into four categories:

### Core Growth Engines
Large retail markets with strong growth and substantial digital payment activity.

### Digitally Mature Markets
Economically large states where digital payments are already widely integrated into retail transactions.

### Emerging Growth Markets
States showing strong retail sector growth but relatively lower digital payment usage.

### Low Adoption & Low Maturity States
States where both retail sector size and digital payment adoption remain comparatively lower.

---

## Key Observations

The analysis highlights several important patterns:

- Retail GSVA growth existed across many states **before the introduction of UPI**.
- Higher UPI transaction activity is concentrated in **economically larger states**.
- No consistent relationship is observed between **higher UPI transaction share and higher retail GSVA growth**.
- Digital payments appear to **complement existing economic activity rather than act as a primary driver of retail sector expansion**.
